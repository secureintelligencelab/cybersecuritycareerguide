üéØ Essential Tips for Success

üöÄ Getting Started

üí° Start with IT Fundamentals: If you're new to IT, begin with basic networking, operating systems, and general IT concepts before diving into security.
üí° Build a Home Lab: Set up VirtualBox or VMware with different operating systems. Practice with tools like Wireshark, Nmap, and Metasploit.
üí° Join Communities: Participate in forums like Reddit r/cybersecurity, Discord servers, and local cybersecurity meetups.

üìö Learning Strategy

üí° Hands-On Practice: Theory is important, but practical skills are crucial. Use platforms like TryHackMe, HackTheBox, and VulnHub.
üí° Follow the 70/20/10 Rule: 70% hands-on practice, 20% learning from others (mentors, peers), 10% formal training.
üí° Stay Current: Follow security news through Krebs on Security, Dark Reading, and The Hacker News.

üíº Career Development

üí° Network Actively: Attend conferences (RSA, Black Hat, DEF CON), join professional organizations like (ISC)¬≤ and ISACA.
üí° Document Your Journey: Keep a portfolio of projects, write blog posts about what you learn, and maintain an active LinkedIn profile.
üí° Continuous Learning: Technology evolves rapidly. Plan to spend 10-15% of your time learning new skills and technologies.

üéØ Interview Preparation

üí° Know the Basics: Be solid on networking fundamentals, common attack vectors, and security frameworks.
üí° Prepare Stories: Have specific examples of problems you've solved, even from lab environments or personal projects.
üí° Ask Good Questions: Show genuine interest by asking about the company's security challenges and culture.
